## Page Content

### Introduction

Dr. Imran Qureshi offers a full range of minimally invasive, interventional pain management treatments at his Katy, TX office. Every treatment plan begins with an accurate diagnosis — understanding the exact source of your pain — followed by a personalized approach that targets that source directly.

Our goal is to reduce your pain, restore your function, and help you avoid surgery whenever possible. Most procedures are performed in-office under fluoroscopic (live X-ray) guidance, take less than an hour, and allow you to return home the same day.

### Interventional Procedures

**Epidural Steroid Injections**
Targeted anti-inflammatory medication delivered to inflamed nerve roots in the spine. One of the most effective nonsurgical treatments for herniated discs, spinal stenosis, sciatica, and radiculopathy. Fluoroscopy-guided for precision.
→ [Learn more about epidural steroid injections](/treatments/epidural-steroid-injections)

**Radiofrequency Ablation (RFA)**
Uses targeted heat energy to disrupt pain signals from specific nerves, providing relief that can last 6 months to 2 years. Especially effective for facet joint pain, sacroiliac joint pain, and chronic neck or back pain that has responded to diagnostic nerve blocks.
→ [Learn more about radiofrequency ablation](/treatments/radiofrequency-ablation)

**Nerve Blocks**
Targeted anesthetic and anti-inflammatory injections to specific nerves, serving both diagnostic and therapeutic purposes. Types include medial branch blocks, selective nerve root blocks, occipital nerve blocks, sympathetic blocks, and peripheral nerve blocks.
→ [Learn more about nerve blocks](/treatments/nerve-blocks)

**Joint Injections**
Anti-inflammatory injections into facet joints, sacroiliac joints, and peripheral joints (knee, hip, shoulder) to reduce pain from arthritis, injury, or inflammation. Performed under fluoroscopic or ultrasound guidance.
→ [Learn more about joint injections](/treatments/joint-injections)

**Trigger Point Injections**
Targeted injections into painful muscle knots (myofascial trigger points) to relieve chronic muscle pain, tension headaches, and myofascial pain syndrome. Quick in-office procedure with minimal downtime.
→ [Learn more about trigger point injections](/treatments/trigger-point-injections)

**Spinal Cord Stimulation (SCS)**
An implantable device that sends mild electrical pulses to the spinal cord, interrupting pain signals before they reach the brain. For patients with chronic pain that has not responded to other treatments, including failed back surgery syndrome, CRPS, and neuropathy. A trial period allows you to test the device before permanent implantation.
→ [Learn more about spinal cord stimulation](/treatments/spinal-cord-stimulation)

### Regenerative Medicine

**PRP Therapy (Platelet-Rich Plasma)**
Uses concentrated platelets from your own blood to promote tissue repair and reduce inflammation. Treats chronic joint pain, tendon injuries, mild to moderate osteoarthritis, and degenerative disc conditions. A natural, biologic approach to healing.
→ [Learn more about PRP therapy](/treatments/prp-therapy)

### Diagnostic Procedures

**Discography (Provocative Discogram)**
A diagnostic procedure that identifies which intervertebral disc is causing your chronic back pain. By pressurizing each disc and evaluating your pain response, discography provides the diagnostic precision that MRI alone cannot. Used to guide surgical planning and advanced treatment decisions.
→ [Learn more about discography](/treatments/discography)

**Diagnostic Imaging**
Comprehensive imaging services including X-ray and fluoroscopy to support accurate diagnosis and guide interventional procedures. Advanced imaging is the foundation of precise, safe treatment.
→ [Learn more about our imaging services](/imaging)

### Spine Procedures

**Kyphoplasty**
A minimally invasive procedure to treat vertebral compression fractures caused by osteoporosis, trauma, or cancer. A balloon is used to restore vertebral height, and bone cement stabilizes the fracture. Most patients experience significant pain relief within 48 hours.
→ [Learn more about kyphoplasty](/treatments/kyphoplasty)

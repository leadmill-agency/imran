What Insurance Typically Covers

Most major insurance plans — including Medicare — cover interventional pain management procedures when medically necessary. Covered services typically include:

  • ✓Epidural steroid injections — for herniated discs, sciatica, spinal stenosis
  • ✓Radiofrequency ablation (RFA) — for facet joint arthritis, chronic back & neck pain
  • ✓Nerve blocks — diagnostic and therapeutic nerve blocks
  • ✓Joint injections — knee, hip, shoulder, sacroiliac joint
  • ✓Spinal cord stimulation — trial and permanent implant (prior auth required)
  • ✓Office consultations — initial evaluation and follow-up visits
  • ✓Diagnostic imaging — X-ray and MRI interpretation

PRP (platelet-rich plasma) therapy is typically not covered by insurance as it is considered investigational by most carriers. Ask our team about self-pay pricing.

Medicare & Medicare Advantage

Dr. Qureshi is a participating Medicare provider. Traditional Medicare Part B covers most pain management procedures — including epidural steroid injections, radiofrequency ablation, nerve blocks, and joint injections — when medically documented and necessary.

Important: Medicare requires documentation of prior conservative treatment (physical therapy, medications) and supporting imaging (MRI or X-ray) before authorizing interventional procedures. Dr. Qureshi will review your history and ensure everything is properly documented to support your claim.

Workers’ Compensation

If you were injured on the job and are receiving workers’ compensation benefits in Texas, our office treats work-related musculoskeletal injuries, spine injuries, and chronic pain conditions. We work directly with your claims adjuster and case manager.

Self-Pay & Uninsured Patients

We offer competitive self-pay rates for consultations and procedures. Our team will provide a clear cost estimate before any visit or procedure. We want cost to never be a barrier to quality pain care.

Insurance FAQs

Does Dr. Qureshi accept my insurance? +
We accept most major commercial insurance plans, Medicare, and select Medicaid and marketplace plans. Call (281) 982-2144 with your insurance card and our billing team will verify your specific benefits within one business day.
Do I need a referral to see Dr. Qureshi? +
This depends on your specific plan. PPO plans generally do not require a referral. HMO plans typically require one from your primary care physician. We’ll confirm referral requirements when we verify your insurance.
How do I find out my deductible and copay? +
When you call to schedule, our team will contact your insurance company and provide a clear breakdown: your deductible, how much has been met for the year, your specialist copay or coinsurance, and out-of-pocket maximum information.
Does insurance cover epidural steroid injections? +
Yes — epidural steroid injections are covered by most major commercial plans and Medicare when medically necessary. Coverage typically requires documented conservative treatment and supporting imaging. We handle prior authorization in-house.
Is PRP therapy covered by insurance? +
PRP therapy is currently considered investigational by most commercial carriers and Medicare and is typically not covered. Ask our team about self-pay pricing for PRP procedures.
What if my insurance claim is denied? +
Our billing team handles denials and appeals on your behalf. We have extensive experience navigating prior authorization and medical necessity reviews. Most denials are overturned on appeal with proper documentation, which we provide.
